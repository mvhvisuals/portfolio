Tortilla Española

Let’s dive into the Tortilla Española! This iconic potato omelet is a staple in Spanish cuisine, enjoyed as a tapa, main dish, or even a quick snack. Tortilla Española pairs wonderfully with pan con tomate, a fresh salad, or jamón ibérico. For drinks, a crisp Albariño or a light Tempranillo complements its rich, savory flavors beautifully. Now, let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 1 ¾ cup olive oil, divided
  • 1 large yellow onion, chopped
  • 7 russet potatoes, cut into ⅛ inch slices
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt
  • 6-7 eggs

Steps

  1. Heat 1½ cups olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onions, potatoes, and 1 teaspoon salt.
  2. Cook, pressing gently with a spatula, until potatoes are fork-tender but not mushy. Transfer to a bowl to cool slightly.
  3. In a medium bowl, beat eggs with ½ teaspoon salt.
  4. Add potatoes and onions to the eggs, mixing well. If needed, add another egg to ensure some extra at the top.
  5. Heat remaining ¼ cup olive oil in a skillet over medium-low heat. Pour in the mixture, flatten, and let cook undisturbed until set.
  6. Place a plate over the skillet, flip the tortilla onto it quickly, then slide it back into the pan, uncooked side down.
  7. Cook until the tortilla slides easily in the pan. Serve from the skillet or flip onto a plate.
